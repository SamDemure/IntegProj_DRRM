Typhoon

Typhoons, also known as hurricanes or cyclones in different regions, are powerful tropical storms characterized by strong winds, heavy rainfall, storm surges, and potentially devastating impacts. These storms form over warm ocean waters, gaining strength as they move across the water and can cause widespread damage when they make landfall.

Typhoons form in tropical regions when warm ocean waters fuel the development of low-pressure systems, generating powerful winds that spiral around a calm center, known as the eye.

These storms bring heavy rainfall, leading to flooding, landslides, and storm surges that can inundate coastal areas. The strong winds can cause structural damage, uproot trees, and disrupt power and communication lines.
